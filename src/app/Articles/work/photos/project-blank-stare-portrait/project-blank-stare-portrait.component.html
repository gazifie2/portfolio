The blank stare

Back story:
This was shot during the preparation to film some interviews for a mini documentary.
We were setting up the lights for the scene, and I was the test subject to test the lighting.
Below are some of the settings for Geeks:

ISO: 800 Focal ratio: F/2.2 Shutter speed: 1/50 Focal length: 50mm

This picture needed some work, I had a bad hair day, I needed some skin care, and there were some issues with shadow cast.

Work done

Skin retouching: I removed the dark spots without losing the shine and skin details.

Eliminate hair frizz: Not everyday is a good hair day, especially the morning after a night shower. I had a bad frizzy hair, so I had to clean things up. Beside that, I gave my beard some love.

Color grading: Orange and teal are the opposite colors on the color wheel (complementary colors), thus it creates more contrast between the subject and the background.
Orange is more like the color of the skin, so I made the background more blueish to add depth to the picture.

Fixing shadow cast: This picture was taken during the setup of the lights, there was a pretty bad shadow cast from the nose, on the cheek and eye.
So I had to fix it in post, using cloning, dodging and burning techniques.

Details you might have missed

Cloning and repainting: Trimming the beard on top of the shirt required repainting lost details of the shirt.

Eyes brightening: Often we lose the shine of the eyes in pictures, so it's nice to add back some life to eyes and draw more attention to them.

Cleaning the background : Removing the writing in the background to eliminate distraction.

Déjà-vu: You already seen this picture in the welcome page.
Why? Because I wanted to showcase one of the hardest things to do when photo editing: Removing background behind hair (or beard for that matter).
